AI Disruption & Automation Risk Analysis

Tableau R Data Visualization
🤖

Overview

Analyzed workforce data to explore how AI and automation may reshape occupations by 2030. This managerial analytics study combines Tableau dashboards with statistical validation in R to examine how job structure and skills relate to automation exposure.

The analysis reveals that skill complexity, not education level or years of experience, predicts job resilience in the face of AI-driven automation.

Key Findings

🧠
Skills Over Credentials
Skill complexity predicts job resilience better than education level or experience

Technical Approach

1. Data Collection & Analysis

  • Analyzed workforce data across multiple industries and job categories
  • Mapped job tasks to automation susceptibility metrics
  • Evaluated skill requirements and complexity levels

2. Visual Analytics in Tableau

  • Built interactive dashboards showing automation risk by occupation
  • Created visualizations of skill complexity vs. automation exposure
  • Designed filters for exploring patterns by industry, education, experience

3. Statistical Validation in R

  • Performed statistical tests to validate automation risk patterns
  • Analyzed correlation between job characteristics and resilience
  • Tested hypothesis that skill complexity predicts job security

Tools & Technologies

Tableau R Statistical Testing Data Visualization Dashboard Design Predictive Analytics

Strategic Implications

This analysis provides guidance for workforce planning and career development:

  • Focus on developing complex, non-routine skills to remain resilient
  • Education credentials alone don't guarantee protection from automation
  • Organizations should prioritize reskilling for AI-adjacent roles
  • Career planning should emphasize skill development over tenure accumulation
